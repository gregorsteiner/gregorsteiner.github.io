## About me

I am a first-year PhD student at the University of Warwick, supervised by [Prof. Mark Steel](https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/sci/statistics/staff/academic-research/steel/). My research interests include causal inference and model uncertainty, with a particular focus on economic and biomedical applications. Currently, I am working on Bayesian model averaging and model selection in instrumental variable models.

Before starting my PhD, I earned an MSc in Economics from WU Vienna and a BSc in Statistics from the University of Vienna. During this time, I gained valuable experience working at the Centre for Medical Data Science at the Medical University of Vienna, where I developed and maintained statistical software.
